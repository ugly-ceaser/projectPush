Privacy Statement

This statement declares how Umuahia Community Ireland collects personal information through its website and how such information is used. Why do we collect personal data?

Personal Data means any data related to a person that allows that person to be identified, such as name, age, telephone number, e-mail, and postal address.
We want to collect, maintain, and use your Data to deliver the service you have requested via our website. Your information will only be used to match your request for information with our services.
We will use your Data only for the purpose outlined above. We have in place stringent policies and terms on Data use and protection.

How do we collect personal data?

We collect data via our website contact form and email link.

Security of collected data

We employ all reasonable measures to ensure the security and quality of your data.

How do we protect your data?

You have the right to be informed about why, how, and by whom your data is processed. You have the right to access your data, rectify inaccurate data, and have your data erased. If you would like a copy of the personal data we store about you, please get in touch with us using the details below. To ensure privacy, you will be asked to provide some proof of identification.

Your Data Rights

Internal access to your data is limited to those individuals who will directly supply you with information and services. We employ the necessary physical, electronic, and procedural safeguards to comply with regulations to protect your data.

Industry Standards

Our privacy policy has been established following the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Irish laws, the Data Protection Acts 1988 & 2003 along with the Statutory Instrument Number 535 of 2003 European Communities (Electronic Communications Networks and Services) (Data Protection and Privacy) Regulations 2003 (“SI 535/2003”)
